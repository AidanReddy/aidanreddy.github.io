Aidan Patrick Reddy

I am a PhD student in theoretical condensed matter physics at MIT, where I began in fall of 2021. My research focuses on unusual behaviors of strongly interacting electrons in quantum materials. For example, I study fractional Chern insulators, in which electrons split intofractions of themselves, and Wigner crystals, in which electrons freeze into a grid due to their mutual repulsion. I often use computational tools like exact diagonalization and variational Monte Carlo. I enjoy building these tools and applying them to exciting problems.

Before starting my PhD, I completed my undergraduate degree in physics and math at Columbia University in spring 2021. I am originally from Toledo, Ohio. Outside of physics, I enjoy running, hiking, and playing guitar.
